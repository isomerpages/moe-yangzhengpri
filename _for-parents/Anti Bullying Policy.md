<h1><strong>Anti-Bullying Policy</strong></h1>
<p><strong><u>Our Commitment</u></strong>
</p>
<p>At Yangzheng Primary School, we believe every child deserves to feel safe,
valued, competent and empowered in their learning journey. We are committed
to creating a caring and enabling school environment where all students
can flourish and develop positive relationships with their peers.</p>
<p>Our approach focuses on teaching kindness, empathy, and respect whilst
addressing any hurtful behaviour that may arise. We work together as a
school community – staff, students, and parents – to ensure everyone feels
they belong.</p>
<p><strong><u>What are hurtful behaviours and bullying?</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Hurtful behaviours are unkind and hurtful acts such as once-off insensitive
comments, actions and physical fights, while bullying involves persistent
and intentional hurtful acts.</p>
<p>Hurtful behaviours and bullying can take various forms, including verbal,
social, physical and cyber:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Verbal: Name-calling, spreading rumours,
or making threats</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Social exclusion: Deliberately leaving
someone out, or encouraging others to ignore someone</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Physical actions: Pushing, hitting, or
damaging someone's belongings</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Cyber bullying: Using online technology
like phones, computers, or social media to hurt or embarrass others on
purpose and repeatedly</p>
<p><strong><u>The school’s firm stance against hurtful behaviours</u></strong>
</p>
<p><strong>The school takes a firm stance against all forms of hurtful behaviours</strong>,
from once-off, insensitive comments to persistent intentional acts of bullying
and physical violence. This stance guides our school in managing different
forms of hurtful behaviours.</p>
<p>We recognise that children may have disagreements or say things they do
not mean. We help students understand the difference between occasional
conflicts (which are normal) and persistent bullying behaviour, addressing
each situation with care and appropriate support.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong><u>How We Prevent Incidents</u></strong>
</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Take a firm stance against</strong> hurtful
behaviours and bullying</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Foster a caring and enabling school environment</strong>,
with positive teacher-student relationships and strong peer support structure
with trained student leaders</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Conduct Character &amp; Citizenship Education lessons</strong> on
empathy, conflict management, resilience and cyber wellness</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Have regular communications</strong> with
staff and students</p>
<p><strong><u>How to Report Concerns</u></strong>
</p>
<p>We want parents and students to feel comfortable coming to us with any
concerns. You can reach out to us in several ways:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Direct reporting to any staff member</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Reporting boxes that are located
outside the General Office, at the Student Leadership notice board or outside
the Student Services Hub</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Online confidential reporting form
via the school website</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Parent contact with teachers/school
leaders</p>
<p><strong><u>How We Respond</u></strong>
</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Immediate physical and psychological safety</strong> measures
for all students involved</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Prompt investigation</strong> and
timely updates, subject to case complexity</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Consequences, </strong>including
disciplinary actions, conduct grade adjustments, learning prosocial skills
and reconciliation of relationships</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Support plans</strong> for affected
students, including victims and offender</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Restorative support</strong> including
school counselling, external referrals and community resources</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Partnership with parents</strong> throughout
the process, including informing parents at the earliest opportunity</p>
<p><strong><u>How Parents Can Support</u></strong>
</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Talk regularly</strong> with
your child about school experiences and friendships</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Teach respectful behaviour</strong> and
reinforce healthy digital habits at home</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Monitor</strong> your child’s
online and offline interactions and activities</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Share concerns promptly</strong> with
your child’s Form Teacher or Year Head</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Work collaboratively</strong> with
school on agreed interventions</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Model positive behaviour</strong> in
your own interactions, such as being calm and respectful in communications</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Use 'teachable moments'</strong> in
your daily interactions with your child to explain why certain conduct
is inappropriate</p>
